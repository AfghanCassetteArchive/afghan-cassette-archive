A living archive for music that survived.

Cassettes were one of the central ways Afghan music traveled—between cities, across borders, through displacement, and into homes throughout the diaspora.

This archive documents not only sound, but also the handmade artwork, labels, studios, languages, and networks that shaped Afghanistan’s cassette culture.

Materials are shared for educational, historical, research, and archival purposes on a non-commercial basis. Recordings and artwork remain the property of their respective artists, producers, labels, broadcasters, and rights holders. Rights holders may contact the archive to request corrected credit or removal.
